## Resurrecting an Old Machine

I always used to bug my father about being a hoarder. Today, that habit finally paid off by giving me the perfect resource to start my homelab.

### Enter HP Pavilion

My parents bought this machine sometime during the second Obama administration. It was meant to replace our old family computer, a laptop I may or may not have bricked after infecting it with ransomware while trying to download a Minecraft mod (whoops😅).

The PC had been gathering dust for a few years, and I was curious to see if it still worked. I cleaned it up and surprisingly it boot into Windows. Here is the computer and its specs:

![HP Pavilion 23 AiO recovered from storage](/assets/img/posts/2025-09-04-homelab-v1-1/pavilion-recovered.jpg){: w="700" h="400" }_HP Pavilion AiO recovered from storage_

| Spec     | Value                            |

| CPU      | Intel Core i5-4590T @ 2.00 GHz   |
| Memory   | 8 GB DDR3 @ 1600 MHz             |
| Storage  | WD Blue 1 TB 7200 RPM HDD        |
| Graphics | Intel HD Graphics 4600           |
| Wi-Fi    | Intel Dual Band Wireless-AC 3160 |

### Quick Upgrade

Since the machine was still functional, I decided to give it a small refresh with two upgrades:

- An additional 8 GB stick of RAM, bringing the total to 16 GB
- A 1 TB SSD to replace the decade-old hard drive

### RAM Upgrade

Installing the RAM was straightforward, just slot the new stick into the board.

### SSD Upgrade

The storage upgrade was a little more tedious. The drive cage was designed for a full-sized 3.5" HDD, so the slimmer SSD was loose inside the cage. To fix this, I improvised a mount using the SSD packaging and some tape to give it roughly the same profile as the original hard drive.
